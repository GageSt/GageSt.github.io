Diving Down Into the World of Salps

Salps,

frequently referred to as "Sea Squirts" are ocean-dwelling relatives of plankton that reach impressive sizes. These unique creatures live throughout the ocean; some near the equator responding to algae blooms, but mostly in the Southern Ocean living in large Atlantic colonies.

Salp Facts!
Salp colonies are still being studied for their overall impact on oceanic carbon recycling!

Do they sting?

Not at all! Salps are harmless creatures that travel in large numbers of clones.

Salps aren't easy to spot, nearly invisible and soft-bodied, out of water they quickly lose their shape, turning into a sort of "salp jelly"

Large Plankton/Marine Invertebrates

Salps are a large plankton that possess a vertebrae. These creatures mainly feed off small species of Phytoplankton by filtering it through their stomachs as they travel.

Barrel Shaped Bodies

Salps clone themselves horizontally in chains, propelling themselves by pushing water through their barrel shaped bodies like a jet engine. While the chain travels horizontally, the outermost Salp is still considered the "Leader Salp".

Scientists have been making polar expeditions in recent years to study these creatures as they continue to show up on antarctic expeditions.
